# Chapter 4 (Book Ch 3): Myelodysplastic Syndrome (MDS)

> *Pediatric Oncology: A Comprehensive Guide* — Imbach, Kühne, Arceci

**Author**: Thomas Kühne

---

## Definition

MDS is a **heterogeneous group of clonal bone marrow disorders** characterized by:
- Ineffective hematopoiesis
- Normo- or hypercellular bone marrow
- Peripheral cytopenia (one or more cell lines)
- Variable risk of transformation to **acute myelogenous leukemia (AML)**

> **Key distinction**: Myeloproliferative syndrome (MPS) has predominantly proliferative features with minor/absent dysplasia; MDS has dysplasia with cytopenias. Pathogenesis is likely different — WHO separates them.

---

## Classification

### FAB Classification (Pediatric MDS by FAB Criteria)

| Disease | Children (%) | Adults (%) | Median Age | Blasts (PB) | Blasts (BM) |
|---------|------------|------------|------------|-------------|-------------|
| Refractory anemia (RA) | 20 | 28 | 6 years | <1% | <5% |
| RA with ringed sideroblasts (RARS) | <1 | 24 | — | <1% | <5%, RS ≥15% |
| RA with excess blasts (RAEB) | 26 | 23 | 7 years | <5% | 5–19% |
| RAEB in transformation (RAEB-T) | 28 | 9 | 26 months | ≥5% or Auer rods | 20–29% |
| Chronic myelomonocytic leukemia (CMML) | 20 | 16 | 9 months | Monocytes ≥1×10⁹/l | <20% |

### WHO Classification

| Disease | Peripheral Blood | Bone Marrow |
|---------|----------------|-------------|
| RA | Anemia, no/rare blasts | Erythroid dysplasia, <5% blasts |
| RARS | Anemia, no blasts | ≥15% ringed sideroblasts, <5% blasts |
| RCMD | Cytopenia, no/rare blasts | Dysplasia ≥10% in ≥2 myeloid lines |
| RAEB-1 | Cytopenia, <5% blasts | 5–9% blasts |
| RAEB-2 | 5–19% blasts, Auer rods± | 10–19% blasts, Auer rods± |
| MDS-U | Cytopenia, rare blasts | Unilineage dysplasia, <5% blasts |
| del(5q) | Anemia, normal/increased Plt | <5% blasts, isolated del(5q) |

### WHO Myelodysplastic/Myeloproliferative Diseases
- Chronic myelomonocytic leukemia (CMML)
- Atypical CML (aCML)
- Juvenile myelomonocytic leukemia (JMML)
- Unclassifiable

## Epidemiology

- 3% of hematologic malignancies of childhood
- If MDS counted as AML precursor: incidence 12–20%
- JMML estimated at 2.5–3% of childhood leukemia
- Incidence likely **underestimated** due to oligo/asymptomatic forms

---

## Predisposing Factors

**Constitutional chromosomal aberrations:**
- Trisomy 8 mosaicism, Trisomy 21 mosaicism/Down syndrome
- Klinefelter syndrome, fragile X syndrome, Turner syndrome

**DNA repair syndromes:**
- Fanconi anemia, Ataxia telangiectasia, Bloom syndrome, Xeroderma pigmentosum

**Other:**
- Neurofibromatosis
- Li-Fraumeni syndrome (p53 mutation)
- Aplastic anemia, Shwachman syndrome, Pearson syndrome
- Alkylating agents and/or radiotherapy exposure

---

## Etiology

- Multistep process with accumulation of genetic lesions
- RAS proto-oncogene mutations and interaction with tumor suppressor *NF1*
- Initial genetic events not yet elucidated

---

## Clinical Manifestations

- No specific findings for morphological categories
- Bone marrow failure: **pallor, skin hemorrhages**
- Hepato-/splenomegaly (especially in RAEB, RAEB-T)
- Constitutional genetic syndromes often present in infants/young children

---

## Laboratory Findings

- Bone marrow usually **hypercellular** (reduced cellularity in 15%)
- Red cell abnormalities: nuclear-cytoplasmic asynchrony, megaloblastic change, Howell-Jolly bodies
- Pelger-Huët nuclear anomaly (hypogranular granulocytes)
- Megakaryocytic abnormalities: micromegakaryocytes
- Cytogenetics: abnormal karyotype in >70% of patients
- Consider MDS if maturation abnormalities + dysplasia in ≥1 cell line

---

## Differential Diagnosis

- Acute leukemia (per FAB/WHO blast percentage)
- Vitamin B12, folate, pyridoxine, riboflavin deficiencies

---

## Treatment

| Approach | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| Conventional chemotherapy | Not curative |
| Myeloablative + stem cell transplantation | Most effective |
| AML-type chemotherapy | Used as induction |
| Long-term survival (post-SCT) | ~**40%** |

Remains highly controversial due to disease heterogeneity.
