# Chapter 6: Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma (NHL)

> *Pediatric Oncology: A Comprehensive Guide* — Imbach, Kühne, Arceci

**Author**: Paul Imbach

---

## Definition

NHL is **neoplasia of the lymphatic system and precursor cells** with:
- Genetically disturbed regulation of proliferation, differentiation, and apoptosis
- Morphologically and cytogenetically heterogeneous disorders
- Bone marrow involvement ≥25% blasts → classified as **Stage IV** (or leukemia by definition)

---

## Incidence

- 5% of all childhood neoplasias
- ~7 per million children <16 years newly diagnosed/year
- Peak incidence 5–15 years; rare <2 years
- Boys:girls = **2:1**
- Burkitt lymphoma (BL): endemic form in Africa 10/100,000; sporadic 0.2/100,000

---

## Etiology and Molecular Genetics

- Unknown etiology in humans
- Burkitt lymphoma: **t(8;14)** — chromosome 14 immunoglobulin locus + **c-MYC** oncogene (also t(8;2) and t(22;8))
- Burkitt-like (BLL): **t(14;18)** on *BCL6*

**Predisposing conditions:**
- Acquired immunodeficiency (HIV, autoimmune disorders)
- Epstein-Barr virus (endemic BL, lymphoproliferative syndrome)
- Congenital B-cell defects: X-linked agammaglobulinemia, selective IgA/M deficiency
- Congenital T-cell defects: thymus hyperplasia
- Bloom syndrome, Chédiak-Higashi, SCID, ataxia telangiectasia, Wiskott-Aldrich
- Post-immunosuppressive treatment or hydantoin

---

## WHO Classification

| Histology | Rate | Immunophenotype | Main Location |
|-----------|------|----------------|---------------|
| **Burkitt/Burkitt-like** | **50%** | B-cell | Abdomen |
| Large B-cell (LCBL) | 7–8% | B-cell | Abdomen, nodes, skin, bone |
| **Lymphoblastic (LL)** | **30%** | Pre-T or Pre-B | Thorax, lymph nodes, bone |
| Anaplastic large-cell (ALCL) | 7–8% | T-cell | Nodes, skin, soft tissue, bone |
| Unclassifiable | <5% | Non-T-cell | Variable |

## Histological Characteristics

### Burkitt Lymphoma (BL) — 50%
- Large vacuolated cells, fine nuclear chromatin, 2–5 nucleoli
- **"Starry sky"** pattern (L3 morphology — see Ch 2)
- B-cell with IgM surface expression (kappa or lambda)
- High Ki-67, CD19/20/22/77/79a+, CD10/38±, TdT−
- 80% have **t(8;14)** or t(8;2) or t(22;8) → c-MYC on chr8q24
- 40% with p53 mutation

### Large B-Cell Lymphoma (LCBL) — 7–8%
- Large cells, frequently lobulated nucleus, prominent nucleoli
- B lymphocytes: CD20/22/38+; surface Ig−; TdT−
- Translocation with BCL2 and BCL6; 5–10% with t(8;14)

### Lymphoblastic Lymphoma (LL) — 30%
- Morphologically **indistinguishable from ALL**
- Uniform small cell population, high nuclear:cytoplasmic ratio
- T-precursor: CD1/2/3/5/7/8+, TdT+
- Mediastinal enlargement (thymus) usually present
- Translocations: t(11;14), t(1;14), t(8;14), t(10;14)

### Anaplastic Large-Cell Lymphoma (ALCL) — 7–8%
- Predominantly anaplastic cells
- **CD30+**, CD15−/45−/+, EMA+, T-cell receptor (Tβ/Tδ)
- Frequently **t(2;5)** — *ALK* gene involved

---

## Clinical Manifestations

**General symptoms**: fatigue, nausea, anorexia, loss of weight, fever (duration days to weeks)

**By location:**

| Site | Symptoms |
|------|---------|
| **Abdomen** (BL) | Ileocecal pain, vomiting, obstruction, ascites, appendicitis-like |
| **Mediastinum** (LL) | Cough, stridor, dyspnea, wheezing; SVC syndrome |
| **Peripheral nodes** | Cervical, supraclavicular, inguinal — firm, non-tender |
| **Other** | CNS, skin, orbit, parotid, gonads, epidural |

---

## Differential Diagnosis

**Among NHL forms:**
- Sporadic BL: abdomen 25%, bone marrow 20–40%, CNS rare
- Endemic BL (Equatorial Africa): jaw 70% in <5 years
- LL vs ALL: >25% bone marrow blasts = ALL (stage IV)

**Other disorders:**
- Lymph node enlargement in infections
- Autoimmune lymphoproliferative syndrome (ALPS)
- Hodgkin disease (slower progression, lower LDH)
- Neuroblastoma/sarcoma metastases

---

## Diagnosis

- Histology from lymph node, peripheral blood, bone marrow, or effusion fluid
- Immunophenotype + molecular cytogenetics required
- Serum **LDH** monitors tumor progression/response
- Serum **uric acid** — risk of nephropathy (tumor lysis)
- **CSF analysis** — NHL in ~10%
- Imaging: US, CT thorax/abdomen, MRI for CNS/abdominal, PET

---

## Staging (Murphy/St. Jude System)

| Stage | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| **I** | Single tumor (extranodal) or single nodal area — not mediastinum/abdomen |
| **II** | Single extranodal tumor + regional nodes; or same side of diaphragm |
| **III** | Both sides of diaphragm; all intrathoracic; all intra-abdominal unresectable |
| **IV** | Any of above + CNS or bone marrow involvement (<25%) |

> Stages I+II = 10–20% of all NHL; **Stages III+IV = 80–90%**

---

## Treatment

### General Principles

- Rapid growth: tumor doubling time <28 hours — treat **immediately**
- **Tumor lysis syndrome** prevention:
  - Hydration 3,000 ml/m²/24h IV
  - Alkalinization (bicarbonate)
  - Allopurinol 10 mg/kg/day; **rasburicase** for severe hyperuricemia

### BL, BLL, and LCBL
- Intensive chemo for 3–6 months:
  - Vincristine + corticosteroids + cyclophosphamide/ifosfamide + doxorubicin + etoposide + **high-dose methotrexate** (5 g/m²) or high-dose ara-C
  - CNS prophylaxis: intrathecal chemo (no CNS irradiation)
- **Overall survival: 70–90%**

### Lymphoblastic Lymphoma (LL)
- As in ALL: induction → consolidation → CNS prophylaxis → maintenance
- Duration: 1–3 years
- **Prognosis: 80–90%** long-term survival

### ALCL
- Therapy as BL/LCBL or LL protocols
- 80% long-term survival

### Relapse
- BL/BLL/LCBL: ICE (ifosfamide, cisplatin, etoposide) + high-dose chemo + auto/allo SCT
- Rituximab (CD20 monoclonal antibody) as adjuvant
- Relapsing LL: allogeneic SCT (as in ALL relapse)
