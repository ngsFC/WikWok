# Chapter 11: Nephroblastoma (Wilms Tumor)

> *Pediatric Oncology: A Comprehensive Guide* — Imbach, Kühne, Arceci

**Author**: Paul Imbach

---

## Definition

- **Malignant embryonal tumor of renal tissue**
- First described in 1899 by Max Wilms as "Mischgeschwülste der Niere" (mixed tumors of the kidney)

---

## Incidence

- **6% of all neoplasias** in children
- ~8 per million children <16 years annually
- **78% of children with nephroblastoma are <5 years old**
- Peak incidence: **2nd–3rd year of life**
- Congenital form at delivery or neonatal period
- Rare in adolescents and adults
- Boys slightly > girls

---

## Chromosomal Association

### Genetic Loci
| Locus | Gene | Association |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Chromosome 11p13 | **WT1** | 10–30% of nephroblastomas |
| Chromosome 11p15 | **WT2** | — |
| Chromosome 17q | FWT-1 | Familial Wilms tumor |
| Chromosome 19q | FWT-2 | Familial Wilms tumor |
| Chromosomes 16q, 1p, 7p, 17q | *TP53* | Unfavorable histology |

### Associated Congenital Syndromes

| Syndrome | Features |
|---------|---------|
| **WAGR** | **W**ilms tumor, **A**niridia, **G**enital malformations, mental **R**etardation; del(11p13) |
| **Denys-Drash** | Pseudohermaphroditism, glomerulopathy, *WT1* mutation (11p); high Wilms risk |
| **Beckwith-Wiedemann (BWS)** | Hemihypertrophy, macroglossia, omphalocele, visceromegaly; *WT2* (11p15) — 15% develop Wilms |
| Isolated hemihypertrophy | Increased Wilms risk |
| Neurofibromatosis | Associated |
| Perlman syndrome | Associated |
| Simpson-Golabi-Behmel | Associated |

**Familial Wilms**: 1–2% of nephroblastomas; chromosomal anomalies + familial gene loci; bilateral in some; increased risk in homozygous twins.

---

## Pathology

### Macroscopic Features
- High variation in size; lobular structure; gray to pink; capsulated
- Occasional cysts and hemorrhages
- Growth into renal vein
- **Bilateral 5–7.5%; multifocal 12% in one kidney**

### Microscopic Features
**Classic (favorable histology):** Mixed epithelial, blastemic, and stromal cellular components

- **Anaplastic nephroblastoma (5%)**: focal or diffuse anaplasia, large atypical nuclei, abnormal mitoses — **unfavorable histology**
- **Clear-cell sarcoma (3%)**: polygonal cells with crystal-clear cytoplasm; del(22q11-12); high bone/lung metastases; age <2 years in 85%; high relapse rate
- **Rhabdoid nephroblastoma (2%)**: acidophilic cytoplasm; metastatic spread to posterior fossa of neurocranium
- **Mesoblastic nephroma (congenital form)**: mean age 2 months; t(12;15)(p13;q25) — similar to infantile fibrosarcoma; mostly benign

**Nephrogenic residual tissue:**
- Found in 1% of all small children autopsies and majority of bilateral nephroblastoma
- Hyperplastic nephrogenic tissue disappears under chemotherapy

---

## Clinical Manifestations

- **Visible and palpable abdominal mass** (most common presenting symptom)
- Palpation with care — risk of **tumor rupture** or dissemination
- Unclear febrile episodes, anorexia, vomiting
- Micro- or macrohematuria in **20–25%**
- **Hypertension** (renin-producing tumor cells)
- Rarely: secondary polycythemia (erythropoietin production)
- Varicocele, inguinal hernia, acute renal failure, coughing/pleural pain (pulmonary metastases)

---

## Laboratory Diagnosis

- Chemistry: exclude renal failure; high serum calcium (rhabdoid nephroblastoma)
- Urine: hematuria; occasionally malignant cells detectable after concentrating
- **Acquired von Willebrand coagulopathy** in ~8% of patients
- 24-h urine catecholamines: to rule out neuroblastoma

---

## Radiological Diagnosis

- **Abdominal US**: first-line (+ CT/MRI with contrast urography)
- Abdominal X-ray: punctuated calcifications in 2–3%; intestinal displacement
- **CT/MRI abdomen** including hepatic area (metastases) + chest CT
- Angiography: for bilateral nephroblastoma
- Radioisotope scans/skeletal survey: if suspected skeletal metastases
- **CNS MRI**: in clear-cell sarcoma or rhabdoid kidney sarcoma; possible brain metastases

---

## Differential Diagnosis

- Multicystic kidney, hydronephrosis, cystic nephroma
- Renal abscess; mesenteric cyst
- Neuroblastoma, rhabdomyosarcoma, **hepatoblastoma**
- Lymphoma of the kidney (rare)
- Renal cell carcinoma
- Congenital mesoblastic nephroma (neonates)

---

## Staging (National Wilms Tumor Study Group)

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **I** | Confined to kidney; completely resected; no capsule penetration or renal sinus vessel involvement |
| **II** | Extends beyond kidney but completely resected (no margins; no LN); penetration of renal capsule, renal sinus vessels, pre-resection biopsy, or local spillage |
| **III** | Gross/microscopic residual tumor postoperatively: inoperable tumor, positive margins, peritoneal spillage, regional LN metastases, transected tumor thrombus |
| **IV** | Hematogenous or distant LN metastases (lung, liver, bone, brain) |
| **V** | **Bilateral renal Wilms tumor at onset** |

---

## Therapy

> Wilms tumor has been transformed from a 90% lethal disease to a **90% cure rate** through collaborative study groups (NWTS, SIOP).

### Surgical Procedures
- **Transabdominal tumor resection** with abdominal exploration including contralateral kidney
- Biopsy of suspected tissue, especially lymph nodes
- If large tumor mass: **preoperative chemotherapy** then surgery (reduces intraoperative rupture risk)
- Primary surgery in infants <6 months and adolescents >16 years
- Avoid biopsy except in unclear presentation (post-biopsy Stage I excluded)

### Chemotherapy

| Setting | Regimen |
|---------|---------|
| Preoperative | Vincristine + actinomycin D (± anthracycline for primary metastases) |
| Postoperative | Duration + drug combination by stage and histology |
| Toxicity | Veno-occlusive disease (VOD) of the liver — especially in infants |

### Radiotherapy
- Nephroblastoma is radiosensitive
- Indicated only in high-risk: Stage III (LN involvement, high malignancy, metastatic spread)
- Start within **first 10 postoperative days**
- Dose: **15–30 Gy** with higher doses to tumor bed and remaining tumors

---

## Therapy in Relapse

**Favorable prognosis (relapse):** ≥6 months after resection, non-irradiated areas, single organ system, no lymph node metastases, no highly malignant variants:
- Vincristine + actinomycin D + doxorubicin + ifosfamide + carboplatin + etoposide

**Poor prognosis (relapse):** High-dose chemotherapy + **autologous SCT**

---

## Prognosis

| Histology | Survival |
|-----------|---------|
| **Favorable histology** | **94–100%** |
| Standard histology | **90%** |
| Unfavorable histology | **70%** |

### Unfavorable Prognostic Factors
- Diffuse anaplasia
- Viable malignant cells after preoperative chemotherapy
- Infiltration of tumor capsule / invasion into vessels
- Non-radical surgical resection
- Lymph node involvement; tumor rupture; metastatic spread
- Large tumor volume; rhabdoid histology
- Molecular: loss of heterozygosity on 1p, 11q, 16q, 22q; *p53* mutations

---

## Subtypes

### Bilateral Wilms Tumor (Stage V)

- Mean age: **15 months** (unilateral: 42 months)
- Association with malformations: 45% (unilateral: 4%)

**Therapy:**
- Preoperative chemo ± RT; individual surgical procedure
- Unilateral nephrectomy + partial resection of contralateral kidney, or bilateral partial resection
- Bilateral resection both kidneys → **renal transplantation** after chemo
- Low-dose RT: 10–20 Gy

**Prognosis:** Usually favorable.

### Congenital Mesoblastic Nephroblastoma (Fetal Renal Hamartoma)
- >80% of neonatal nephroblastomas; 50% of infant nephroblastomas
- Potential malignant cellular variants
- Mostly large abdominal mass after birth until 1 year
- t(12;15)(p13;q25) — similar to infantile fibrosarcoma
- **Therapy:** Nephrectomy; after incomplete resection → therapy as in nephroblastoma if malignant histology suspected

### Renal Cell Carcinoma
- 1–2% in patients >21 years; mainly children >5 years
- Chromosome 3 abnormality
- Symptoms: pain, hematuria, intra-abdominal tumor
- Metastases: lung, liver, regional LN, bone
- **Therapy:** Complete resection; high-risk patients: IFN-α + IL-2 or high-dose radio/chemo (only moderately sensitive)
