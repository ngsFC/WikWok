# Chapter 12: Soft Tissue Sarcoma

> *Pediatric Oncology: A Comprehensive Guide* — Imbach, Kühne, Arceci

**Author**: Paul Imbach

---

## Definition

Soft tissue sarcomas (STS) are a **heterogeneous group of malignant tumors** stemming from muscles, vessels, lymphatic tissue, connective tissue, synovial tissue, or primitive mesenchymal cells.

### Classification by Tissue of Origin

| Tissue | Tumor |
|--------|-------|
| Mesenchyma | Myxoma, mesenchymoma |
| Striated muscle | **Rhabdomyosarcoma** |
| Smooth muscle | Leiomyosarcoma |
| Fatty tissue | Liposarcoma |
| Connective tissue | Fibrosarcoma |
| Synovial tissue | Synovial sarcoma |
| Blood vessels | Angiosarcoma, hemangiopericytoma |
| Lymphatic vessels | Lymphangiosarcoma |
| Nerve sheath | Neurofibrosarcoma (malignant schwannoma) |

### Differential Diagnosis (General)
- Accidental/traumatic/hemorrhagic tumor
- Benign lipoma, myoma, neurofibroma
- Myositis (ossificans, inflammatory)
- Inflammatory myofibrohistiocytosis (pseudosarcoma, pseudotumor of bladder)
- Other neoplasias: NHL, neuroblastoma, Ewing sarcoma, LCH

### Incidence (All STS)
- **7.5% of all childhood neoplasias**
- ~8 per million children <16 years/year
- 70% of children are <10 years at diagnosis; boys:girls = 1.4:1

## Rhabdomyosarcoma (RMS)

**Most common soft tissue sarcoma** — ~50% of all STS in childhood.

### Incidence and Localization

- ~4.3 per million children <16 years/year
- 67% are <10 years at diagnosis; boys:girls = 1.15:1

| Age (years) | Frequency (%) |
|-------------|--------------|
| <1 | 7 |
| 1–4 | 35 |
| 5–9 | 25 |
| 10–14 | 20 |
| >15 | 13 |

| Location | Frequency (%) |
|----------|--------------|
| Head and neck (without orbit) | 26 |
| Orbital | 9 |
| **Genitourinary** | **22** |
| Extremities | 18 |
| Trunk | 7 |
| Retroperitoneal | 7 |
| Perineal and anal | 2 |
| Others | 9 |

### Etiology and Pathogenesis
- Mostly sporadic
- Li-Fraumeni syndrome (*TP53* mutation): high incidence of brain/breast tumors, adrenocortical carcinoma in families with RMS child
- Increased risk in fetal alcohol syndrome; maternal drug use (marijuana, cocaine)
- *NRAS* oncogene abnormalities in 35%

### Histopathology — Four Subtypes

| Subtype | Frequency | Location | Key Features |
|---------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| **Embryonal** | 53–64% | Orbit, head/neck, abdomen, GU | Primitive round cells + spindle cells; eosinophilic cytoplasm; cross-striations 30% |
| Sarcoma botryoides (subtype) | 6% | Vagina, bladder, uterus | Polypoid mass; dense subepithelial cell layer |
| **Alveolar** | 21% | Extremities | Round cells; multinucleated giant cells; fibrotic septa (alveolar structure) — **worst prognosis** |
| Pleomorphic | 1% | Adults mainly | Undifferentiated muscle tissue; spindle cells; variable cytoplasm |
| Undifferentiated | 8% | — | No muscle-specific gene proteins |

### Cytogenetics
- **t(2;13q35;q14)** with 1p36 — changes transcription pattern; *PAX3* and *PAX7* characteristic alterations
- LOH at 11p15 locus (IGF-2 gene)
- Hyperdiploidy (>53 chromosomes): more favorable prognosis than diploid

### Clinical Manifestations by Location

**Head and neck (35%)**: orbit — proptosis (early sign); middle ear — pain, otitis media, polypoid mass; paranasal — sinusitis, obstruction; neck — hoarseness, swallowing difficulty

**Genitourinary (22%)**: urethra, vagina, uterus, prostate, bladder, testes, paratesticular; symptoms: urinary problems, hematuria, vaginal bleeding; 40% with LN involvement (paratesticular)

**Extremities and trunk (25%)**: indolent mass, large at diagnosis; spinal compression; dyspnea (lung metastases)

**Retroperitoneal (7%)**: large tumor at diagnosis; abdominal pain mimicking appendicitis

### Staging (IRS — Intergroup Rhabdomyosarcoma Study)

| Stage | Substage | Description |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| **I** | A | Localized, confined to origin, completely resected |
| | B | Localized, infiltrating beyond origin, completely resected |
| **II** | A | Gross total resection, microscopic residual |
| | B | Regionally extensive (LN+), completely resected |
| | C | Extensive, LN+, gross total but microscopic residual |
| **III** | A | Gross residual after biopsy only |
| | B | Gross residual after major resection (50% debulking) |
| **IV** | — | Distant metastases |

| Stage | Frequency (%) |
|-------|--------------|
| I | 16 |
| II | 20 |
| III | **48** |
| IV | 16 |

**TNM-IRS Prognosis Table:**

| TNM Stage | Prognosis | Sites |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| I | **>90%** | Orbit, head/neck, GU (non-bladder/non-prostate) |
| II | **80–90%** | Bladder/prostate, extremities, cranial parameningeal |
| III | **70%** | Same sites, LN+ or larger tumors |
| IV | **30–40%** | Distant metastases |

### Therapy

**Multidisciplinary approach** based on location, stage, histology.

**Surgical procedure:**
- Total resection if possible without major functional deficits; reduces radiation field
- LN biopsy for extremity/GU/metastatic LN (total regional LN dissection not necessary with combined therapy)

**Radiotherapy:**
- Group I: no irradiation
- Groups II–IV: RT after chemo ± surgery for microscopic/macroscopic residual
- <40 Gy → higher local relapse; pulmonary metastases: 14–18 Gy total + 30 Gy to residual lesions; bone metastases: 50–60 Gy

**Chemotherapy** — all RMS patients (occult micro-metastases common):
- Vincristine + actinomycin D + doxorubicin + ifosfamide + etoposide + cisplatin

**Special locations:**
- **Orbit**: initially biopsy only, >90% EFS; total excision only for relapse/non-response
- **Parameningeal**: radical excision after chemo; CSI if CSF involvement
- **Paratesticular**: orchiectomy ± scrotectomy; intensive therapy for bilateral LN involvement
- **Pelvic**: biopsy → chemo → second-look surgery; radioactive seeds for persistent disease

### Prognosis by Factors
- Favorable: orbit or GU tract (non-prostate), age <7 years
- Unfavorable: alveolar/pleomorphic histology, CNS involvement, extremity with early metastases, age >7 years

**Relapse survival**: Group I 48±12%; Group II 12±9%; Group III 11±5%; Group IV 8±4%

**Secondary tumors:** Of 1,770 IRS study children, 22 developed osteosarcoma, leukemia (AML), or MDS.

## Fibrosarcoma

- **11%** of all STS in childhood; 75% in children <10 years (36% in newborns)
- Boys:girls = 1.2:1

**Location (by frequency):** lower extremities > upper extremities > head/neck > trunk > pelvic > retroperitoneal

**Pathology:**
- Congenital form: uniform fibroblasts/myofibroblasts; low mitosis; t(12;15) — similar to infantile fibrosarcoma
- Classic fibrosarcoma: anaplastic spindle cells in herringbone pattern; collagen; t(x;18), t(2;5), t(7;22); *TP53* mutation → poor prognosis

**Grading:** Grade 1 (well differentiated) → Grade 4 (undifferentiated, high mitosis)

**Treatment:** Primary total resection; RT 60–65 Gy for residual/relapse; chemo as in RMS for Grade 3–4 or metastatic disease

**Prognosis:**
- Grades 1–2: 70% 10-year survival
- Grades 3–4: 30–40% 10-year survival
- Age <5 years: 80% survival, metastatic spread 4–8%; >10 years: 60% survival, metastases 50%

## Synovial Sarcoma

- **5%** of STS in childhood; mainly adolescents and young adults; boys:girls = 1.2:1
- **Location**: extremities 80–90% (thigh, foot, knee, forearm, lower leg, hand); rarely head/neck
- Histology: spindle cells + epithelial cells (PAS-positive polysaccharide and glandular-like structures); keratin antibodies+
- Cytogenetics: **t(x;19)(q11;Xp11)**
- Clinical: painless swelling 60%; calcifications on MRI/CT
- Metastases: lung, LN, bone, rarely brain
- **Therapy**: resection + LN biopsy + RT (50–60 Gy) + chemo (as RMS); without chemo → 75% develop pulmonary metastases within 3 years
- **Prognosis**: Groups I+II: 80% 5-year survival; Groups III+IV: 17% 5-year survival

---

## Liposarcoma

- **4%** of STS in childhood; peak infancy + adolescence; boys:girls = 2:1
- Histological types: well-differentiated (most common, 70%), myxoid (starry cells), round-cell, pleomorphic, mixed
- Cytogenetics: **t(12;16)(q13;p11)**
- Location: thigh, retroperitoneum, head/neck, shoulder, chest
- Metastases: lung, liver; rarely brain, pleura, pancreas, bone
- **Therapy**: radical resection; RT 50–60 Gy (unfavorable histology after subtotal resection); chemo only for undifferentiated forms (as RMS)
- **Prognosis**: well-differentiated: >80% 5-year survival; myxoid: up to 80%; round-cell/pleomorphic: 15–30%

## Malignant Peripheral Nerve Sheath Tumor (MPNST)

- **3–4%** of STS in childhood; **>5% in children with NF1**
- Location: extremities 42%, retroperitoneal 25%, sacral 21%
- Originates from peripheral nerve (plexus, spinal nerve roots); S-100 antibody+
- High association with NF1; 17q11.2 chromosomal alterations; LOH of 17p and 22q
- Clinical: swelling ± pain; rarely pain
- **Therapy**: as Grade III–IV fibrosarcoma and RMS

---

## Leiomyosarcoma

- **<2%** of all solid tumors in childhood; 50% in children <5 years; second peak in adolescence
- Association with HIV infection and other immune deficiencies
- Location: visceral (mainly GI — stomach), GU, retroperitoneal, peripheral soft tissue
- Histology: spindle cells with cigar-shaped nuclei; >10 mitoses/field → prognostically unfavorable
- Clinical: GI — melena, hematemesis, anemia; GU — bleeding, dysuria, visible/palpable tumor
- **Therapy**: radical surgical resection (favorable); combined therapy as RMS (unfavorable histology)
- **Prognosis**: 20–25% 5-year survival overall; favorable location + total resection → better

---

## Hemangiopericytoma

- **3%** of STS in childhood
- Location: (lower) extremities, retroperitoneal, head/neck; infants: tongue + sublingual
- Originates from **vascular pericytes**; difficult to differentiate benign vs malignant
- Cytogenetics: t(12;19)(q13;q13.3), t(13;22)(q22;q11)
- Metastases: mainly lung and bone
- **Therapy**: radical resection; chemo/RT as RMS
- **Prognosis**: 30–70% 5-year survival; children predominance of favorable prognosis
- Congenital variant: subcutaneous; excision sufficient; more favorable

---

## Malignant Fibrohistiocytoma (MFH)

- Rare in childhood; **5–8%** of STS in children
- Karyotypic abnormalities of chromosome 19p+
- Location: extremities, skull, kidney
- Rarely metastatic
- **Therapy**: resection; chemo (as RMS) for advanced/high-mitosis disease
