# Chapter 7: Hodgkin Disease

> *Pediatric Oncology: A Comprehensive Guide* — Imbach, Kühne, Arceci

**Author**: Paul Imbach

---

## Definition

Hodgkin disease is characterized by:
- Progressive, **painless enlargement of lymph nodes** with continuous extension between lymph node regions
- Histological confirmation by **Reed-Sternberg cells**: bi- to multinucleated giant cells, high cytoplasmic content, multilobulated nuclei 15–45 µm — **pathognomonic**
- Cytokine-driven pathogenesis (IL-1, IL-2, IL-3, IL-5, IL-6, IL-9, IL-10, TNF, TGF-α)

---

## Incidence

- 5% of all childhood neoplasias
- ~7 per million children <16 years annually
- Rare <5 years; peak **15–35 years** and after 50
- Boys > girls (equal during adolescence)
- Equal frequency across ethnic groups
- Familial occurrence known; sibling risk 7× higher than expected

---

## Etiology and Pathogenesis

- Epstein-Barr virus correlation
- Genetic predisposition; disturbed humoral and cellular immune response
- Higher socioeconomic status → higher incidence
- High incidence in lupus erythematosus, rheumatoid disorders, ataxia telangiectasia, agammaglobulinemia

---

## Histological Classification

### Classic Hodgkin Disease (T-cell lines predominance)

| Subtype | Rate (%) | Stage I+II (%) | Stage III+IV (%) |
|---------|----------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Nodular-sclerosing** | 54.5 | 60 | 40 |
| Mixed cellular | 32 | 44 | 56 |
| Lymphocyte-predominant | 11.5 | 76 | 24 |
| Lymphocyte-depleted | 2 | 19 | 81 |

- **Nodular-sclerosing**: common in adolescents, mediastinal enlargement, collagen bands — most common
- **Lymphocyte-depleted**: usually advanced stage, unfavorable prognosis
- **Mixed cellular**: pleomorphic with high cellularity; "Hodgkin sarcoma" variant

### Lymphocyte-Predominant Hodgkin Disease (B-cell line)
- **Favorable prognosis**
- Subgroups: lymphocyte-rich and nodular lymphocyte-predominant
- Also called "nodular paragranuloma"

### Immunophenotype

| Phenotype | CD15 | CD20 | CD30 | CD45 | RS cell |
|-----------|------|------|------|------|---------|
| Classic HD | + | ± | + | − | + |
| Lymphocyte-predominant | − | + | − | + | ± |

---

## Ann Arbor Staging Classification

| Stage | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| **I** | Single lymph node region (I) or single extralymphatic organ (I_E) |
| **II** | ≥2 lymph node regions, same side of diaphragm; localized extralymphatic involvement (II_E) |
| **III** | Lymph node regions both sides of diaphragm; ± spleen (III_S) or extralymphatic site (III_E) |
| **IV** | Diffuse/disseminated involvement of ≥1 extralymphatic organ |

### A/B Staging

| | Definition |
|--|-----------|
| **A** | Absence of B symptoms |
| **B** | Fever >38°C (3 consecutive days), night sweats, weight loss >10% in 6 months |

> B symptoms occur in **20–30% of patients**; also sometimes pruritus and/or nausea.

---

## Clinical Presentation

**Lymph nodes**: painless, firm, non-inflammatory; cervical (75%), supraclavicular (25%), axillary (9%), infradiaphragmatic (6%).

**Extranodal:**
- Lung 6%, bone 5%, liver 2%
- Spleen: commonly enlarged (not correlated with disease extension)
- Bone marrow: involvement in B-symptom patients with anemia, low WBC/platelets

**Infections (due to immune deficiency):**
- Herpes zoster, Cryptococcus, Listeria monocytogenes, Diplococcus pneumoniae, Toxoplasma gondii

---

## Laboratory Analyses

**Blood:**
- Anemia → advanced stage or autoimmune hemolysis
- Neutrophilia and eosinophilia in 15–20%
- Lymphocytopenia → advanced disease
- High erythrocyte sedimentation rate (ESR) in active disease

**Chemistry:**
- High serum copper and ferritin

**Immunological:**
- Decreased T-cell function
- Herpes zoster infection in 35% during/after irradiation
- In children: immunological deficits disappear after treatment (vs adults)

---

## Radiological Evaluation

- **Chest**: CT or PET — mediastinal/anterior mediastinum enlargement most common; nodular-sclerosing often correlates with mediastinal involvement
- **Abdomen**: US, MRI, PET
- **Bone**: technetium scintigraphy for bone pain or elevated alkaline phosphatase

---

## Differential Diagnosis

- Toxoplasmosis, tuberculosis, atypical mycobacterial infection
- Non-Hodgkin lymphoma (high LDH, rapid progression)
- Infectious mononucleosis
- Metastatic disease, thymus hyperplasia
- Rheumatoid arthritis, systemic lupus erythematosus
- Sarcoidosis, granulomatous disorder

---

## Treatment

### Chemotherapy Regimens (2–6 courses at monthly intervals)

| Regimen | Drugs | Main Toxicity |
|---------|-------|---------------|
| **(C)OPPA** | Cyclophosphamide + Vincristine (Oncovin) + Procarbazine + Prednisone + Doxorubicin | Hypospermia, cardiomyopathy |
| **OEPA** | Vincristine + Etoposide + Prednisone + Doxorubicin | Cardiomyopathy |
| **ABVD** | Doxorubicin (Adriamycin) + Bleomycin + Vinblastine + Dacarbazine | Lung fibrosis |

### Radiotherapy
- Curative dose: **35–40 Gy** (without chemo); **15–25 Gy** combined with chemo
- Unfavorable: total nodal irradiation or mantle-field irradiation, "inverse Y" below diaphragm
- Favorable: involved-field irradiation above diaphragm

**Favorable prognostic factors:**
- Low number of involved lymph nodes
- No large tumor burden
- No B-staging
- No extranodal manifestation
- Stages I, II, IIIA

---

## Relapse

- Majority within first 3 years after diagnosis
- Second remission rate with combined chemo-/radiotherapy: ~**80%**
- Non-responders: high-dose therapy + SCT
- High risk of **secondary malignancy** after Hodgkin disease

---

## Side Effects and Sequelae

| System | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Thyroid** | Biochemical hypothyroidism after cervical irradiation (TSH elevation); thyroxine substitution needed |
| **Gonads** | Azoospermia (procarbazine); secondary amenorrhea after retroperitoneal irradiation; cryopreserve sperm |
| **Bone** | Growth diminution in irradiated areas; scoliosis |
| **Lungs** | Pneumonitis/pericarditis after mediastinal RT; bleomycin lung fibrosis |
| **Infection** | Splenectomy → life-threatening infections (4% lethality); *Diplococcus, H. influenzae, Streptococci, N. meningitidis*; prevent with penicillin prophylaxis + vaccination |
| **Secondary tumors** | 8–16% within 20–30 years: NHL, leukemia, sarcomas, breast cancer, thyroid cancer |

---

## Prognosis

| Stage | Event-free Survival |
|-------|-------------------|
| Stage I–III | **80–90%** |
| Stage IV | **60–70%** |

**Unfavorable factors**: mediastinal enlargement, B-stage, lymphocyte-depleted histology, adolescent age.

### Follow-up
- Clinical + radiological exam (ultrasound) for 4–5 years
- Thyroid function (after irradiation), echocardiography (after anthracyclines), pulmonary function (after mantle-field RT + bleomycin)
